The Way of Tea

Our Story

Rooted in Tradition,
Grown with Purpose

Midorinami was born from a single cup — one sip that changed everything. After years of searching for matcha that captured the spirit of the Japanese tea ceremony, our founder traveled to the ancient tea fields of Uji, Kyoto, where the finest matcha has been cultivated for over 800 years.

We work directly with small family farms who shade their tea plants for 3 to 4 weeks before harvest — the key step that deepens the flavor and amplifies the vibrant green color. Every tin is stone-ground to order, preserving freshness and the delicate complexity of each leaf.

The Craft

From Field to Bowl

Every step of our process is guided by reverence for the leaf.

01

Shading

Tea plants are covered with bamboo screens for 3–4 weeks before harvest, boosting chlorophyll and amino acids for a sweeter, more complex flavor.

02

Hand-Picking

Only the youngest, most tender leaves — the first and second flush — are carefully hand-selected by experienced farmers at peak harvest.

03

Stone-Ground

Dried leaves (tencha) are slowly ground between granite millstones at low temperature — producing an ultra-fine powder with no heat damage to nutrients.

04

Sealed Fresh

Packed immediately in nitrogen-flushed, light-proof tins to lock in the vibrant flavor, color, and nutritional potency until the moment you brew.
